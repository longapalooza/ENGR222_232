PELTIER COOLER EXPERIMENT

A thermoelectric cooler is a refrigerator that operates on the Seebeck effect, which is an interaction between heat and electricity. For additional information about how this device works, see pages 626-627 in the text (or see the internet). In this lab, you will perform an energy balance on a thermoelectric refrigerator (also known as a Peltier cooler) and calculate the $ COP $ of the thermoelectric device.

To prevent the device from over-heating and to provide a method of measuring the rate of heat transfer, two micro-fluidic channel devices have been created. Both devices are equipped with thermistors that will give us the temperatures of water flowing through the channels at both the inlets and outlets of the device. An energy balance on the water in the micro-fluidic device will help us solve for the heat transfer rates in and out of the Peltier cooler.

We will have full control of the power supplied to the cooler and will be able to easily calculate the electrical power. An energy balance may show flaws in the experimental setup (e.g., heat loss to the surroundings instead of the designated water-channel heat sink). Using any two of the of the heat transfer rates or electrical power, will allow us to determine the coefficient of performance ($ COP $) of the Peltier cooler.
